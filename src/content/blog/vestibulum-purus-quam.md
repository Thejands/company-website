# Small Details That Protect Delivery Timelines

Large project failures often start with small oversights: one missing validation rule, one undocumented dependency, one unresolved assumption before sprint planning.

Great teams build routines that make these issues visible early. They use repeatable QA checks, clear ownership, and milestone reviews tied to outcomes instead of only activity.

## Clear Vision

Detail orientation is not about perfectionism. It is about reducing rework, protecting timelines, and giving stakeholders confidence that progress is real and measurable.
